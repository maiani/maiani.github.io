Andrea Maiani

    Andrea Maiani

    WINQ Postdoctoral fellow at Nordic Institute of Theoretical Physics

    My area of research is at the boundary of condensed matter physics and quantum information. I specialize in quantum nanoelectronic and superconducting heterostructures, where superconducting materials are combined with semiconductors and magnets to generate and control new behaviors not found in nature.

    Research

    Hybrid heterostructures

    My main interest is studying nanoelectronic devices composed of different materials, like superconductors, semiconductors, and magnetic materials. These heterostructures allow achieving properties not available in nature, like topological excitations.

    Superconducting qubits

    I study superconducting circuits to implement quantum information processing. In particular, I study heterogeneous quantum architecture that mixes different types of superconducting qubits to leverage their strengths.

    Vortex nucleation

    The nucleation of vortices in superconductors is an example of a rare event. The frequency of these events is determined by the energy barrier experienced by vortices while crossing the sample boundary. We developed numerical methods to assess the potential landscape of vortices in both conventional and unconventional superconductors.

    Master thesis projects

    On this page you can find a list of available Master thesis projects. Please contact me if you are interested in working on one of these topics or if you have other ideas you want to pursue together. 1. Probing the quantum energy landscape of a superconducting qubit Superconducting qubits rely on Josephson junctions—tiny nonlinear elements whose properties ultimately shape how well a qubit behaves. In theory these junctions create a simple cosine-shaped potential. In practice, real devices often carry small imperfections, such as higher harmonics in the potential, that subtly change the qubit’s energy levels and dynamics.
